§ 04 · The three changes left worth making

If only three things change before v52, these.

Ordered by leverage. None require new prose.

1 · Replace the hero scoreboard with the thesis sentenceThe line "none of the 69 predictions produces a dome-specific result that standard physics cannot already explain" should be the first thing on the page at display scale. The 69 / 0 / 4+4 numbers belong as a small row of figures beneath it, in body type. This single change does more for credibility than every body-text improvement combined.
2 · Turn the verdict distribution into one ordered barYou already state the six categories up front. The matching distribution (22 · 18 · 14 · 9 · 4 · 4) is the document's most transportable single image. Six adjacent stat tiles obscure it; one labeled, severity-ordered bar reveals it.
3 · Make "What Would Change Our Verdict" a persistent overview moduleFour rows. Each: condition · one-line description · current status (0/69, 1 partial, pending Aug 12). Visible on first load. This is the single highest-credibility surface the document can show, and the prose to back it already exists.

After these three, the document and the design will be doing the same job, in the same register. That alignment is the entire point of this review.

§ 05 · Closing note

This kind of revision is what the round-one review hoped for.

The interior of the document has clearly been worked through by someone who took the critique seriously without flinching from it — the plain-language summaries, the explicit verdict-assignment rules, the new "How to Evaluate Any Scientific Claim" section, and the eclipse pre-commitment all read as structural changes, not cosmetic ones. My recommendation has shifted accordingly: from "share privately with the author" to "this is close enough that one more pass on the first viewport makes it citable."

A review that updates itself in response to its own review is, in a small but real way, demonstrating its own thesis. — Closing note, round 2
